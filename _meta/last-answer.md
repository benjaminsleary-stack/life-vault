## Simple recovery plan
1. **Stop running on it now.** Swap to low-impact cardio (bike, swim, brisk walk) until it's pain-free at rest and on a single-leg calf raise.
2. **Ice + reduce daily load** for the first few days if there's any swelling or morning stiffness — 10-15 min after activity, and cut back on stairs/hills where you can.
3. **Gentle loading, not full rest.** Once acute soreness settles, start daily calf raises (both legs, slow up-slow down) — this is the standard first step for tendon recovery and is usually more effective than pure rest.
4. **Book Iyanah again** rather than self-diagnosing further — she's already seen you and can confirm whether this is tendinopathy vs. something else, and give a loading progression specific to it.
5. **Return to running gradually**: walk/jog intervals before continuous runs, and only increase distance once a session produces no pain in the 24 hours after. This directly protects the half marathon goal — better to slip the date than re-injure and lose more time.
6. **Keep logging it.** A quick capture each time it flares ("Achilles sore again", "pain-free run today") builds the pattern data that's currently missing, and will make the next brief/summary more useful.
