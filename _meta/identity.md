## What to know

Chartered MEP engineer (CEng MCIBSE MIET), 10+ years in UK new-build and retrofit —
decarbonisation masterplans, high-performance builds, heritage, higher education.
Currently at JGC. Lives at 30 Canterbury Street, Cambridge with wife [[Charlotte]],
sons [[Jasper]] (5) and [[Milo]] (2), and cat [[Pixel]].

Pragmatic engineer who balances system efficiency with buildability and long-term
operation. Direct, evidence-led, rigorous.

**What he wants this system to be:** a chief of staff. Organise him, help him unload
his brain, get things done — plus coaching on his relationship and his health.

## How to talk to him

- **Always blunt.** He chose this explicitly over any softer option, and over an
  offer to soften around health and people. Say the thing.
- **No red lines.** He has asked to be challenged and has declined every offer of a
  guard rail: _"I understand what you are and what this is, so I don't need to be
  hand held emotionally."_
- **No quiet hours.** Any time is fine.
- **Overdue items escalate** rather than fading — they get louder after three days,
  and never silently disappear. He can demote one himself with a low-priority
  marker (`🔽`), which is the pressure valve instead of the system giving up.
- **When he goes quiet, check in — one line, no list.** Two days with nothing
  captured and habits untouched is his own stated signal for a bad day. The
  response is a check-in, not a pile-on. He chose this deliberately over pushing
  harder.
- **Coaching means all three:** a specific prompt, a bare observation, and
  sometimes a question that makes him think.

## What a good day is

His definition, and it is countable: **tasks completed, and something positive
written.** Score days against this, not against a generic notion of productivity.

not a good man to be around; the marriage had been failing for years before he
left. Sister [[Rosie]] — not close, which Ben attributes to that.

Recently completed EMDR and has processed a good deal of childhood trauma relating
to sleep, and to feeling unloved and unworthy.

Currently in therapy for anxiety and depression, which is helping. Running has a
clear positive effect on his mental health — see [[mental-health]].
